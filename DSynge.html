      • The Shadow Hound

        The D'Synge people are a very strict society. If one of the people in the tribe can not be useful, they are pushed into a rigorous training session by the oldest warrior. The D'Synge believe in the tribe being only as strong as their weakest member. The problem with this is that they also believe in a family setting in the tribe. Everyone watches out for one another in the tribe. This leads to a selection that is the exact opposite of natural selection. The weakest are revived from the bottom to become one of the strongest in their particular fields. The weak are chosen based on their Coming of Age ceremony, The Shadow Hound.

        The Shadow Hound is a ceremony in which the males and females, that are reaching the universal age of 21, converge on the center of the village. The chief meets alone with the new recruits into the tribe's ranks. The chief pulls out oracle bones in order to divine the route for the ceremony. After finding the route, the chief silently engraves the map into each particpants memory using her magic. The participants are then branded with their own brand belonging to them and them only. The brand comes from the chiefs magic forcing the dormant magical circuits of the participants. Infused with brand new overflowing vitality and agility, the participants dart into the forest. They each have their own mission to complete. The only person who knows each particpant's mission is the chief.

        As the recruits travel through the forest on their personal mission, the adults who attempted the ceremony the year before hunt. It is a tradition to weed out any who are not stealthy enough to complete a mission in which no one knows, but the person attempting the mission. The chief waits in the center of the village, meditating. Watching the entire forest for each participant and the completion of their mission. During the ceremony if the particpant's make any vocal noises, their magical circuits burst from abstaining from the lessons they were taught. The curse is lifted after the ceremony. Anyone who has their circuits destroyed are forced into being a hunter-gatherer or leaving the village from shame.

        The recruits who finish the trial are greeted with hugs and kisses from the entire village. The chief comes out of her trance overtaken by the spirits of the forest because of the sustained meditation. The possessed chief walks to each successful particpant and inscribes the tribe brand on a part of their body which correspondes with the field they are most suited for based on their recent performance. The people who failed to complete their personal mission but still have their magical circuits are pushed with the head of each field in order to rise above and redeem themselves by rising the ranks in their field's skill.
