Blossoming Birth

The Earth began as we Aurorians die, alone and empty. The shift under the bosom of the earth shook the crust. Molten rock burst forth from the earth. Spewing what seemed like her pent up aggression, the World cleansed itself once more. Every species, large or small, found itself burnt into a crisp. The World was not merciful in her removal of the trespassers. She reveled in the screams of the burning denizens on her outer skin. The people ran in an attempt to save themselves, sacrificing their "family" is necessary. Such actions only fueled the world's rage. Entire mountains burst forth, raining the melting death into the skies. Raining down from above, the manifestations of mothers anger pierced all in their wake. Man, woman, child, animal, no one was spared.

The rivers, that once rushed down hillsides fueling tribes and towns, no longer ran the life-sustaining water. The many villages and towns centered around rivers and streams were made to be one with the earth again. The barricades created to redirect water to crops were destroyed. Mother's wrath had no limits. She should no sign of ending. The destruction escalated and continued for an eternity. Screams rang out to the heaven for obscene created Gods to help them. Their cries went unanswered for who could answer them? The only God in this world was the World itself. Mother had been caring and bountiful until the distortion of the land started to destroy other lands. How dare people living only because of the grace of mother cause such damage to her? The insolent fools had pushed their luck for far too long and mother found it time to cleanse herself of the infidels.

The time finally came in which the smoke from the burning remnants of the fallen societies broke. The sun creaked through the dark ominous clouds that populated the land. The sun even seemed apprehensive about piercing the dark veil of our Mother's anger. But all things must pass that occurs in the physical world. The outer layer of mother lay bare. Nothing moved. Nothing breathed. Nothing existed. Then came a wind blowing from mother's southern tip. The wind was warm and sweet. The aroma that came with it calmed Mother entirely, closing the openings in which the molten rock once flowed. Following the continuously sweet blowing wind, a bright green light came. The light blinded the entire universe. We had never seen such strength of light. The sun disappeared in the face of the light, conceding it's place as the main source of light if only temporarily.

The bright light seemed to entice mother. Her primal urges rose with the intensity of the light. Growing, blinding, and rising, the light continued to grow. Mother could not deal with the sensations the light gave her. In a burst of energy and love, a wave caressed the world. The waves began slowly, but shifted into a rhythmic pulse. Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh. The pulse grew faster as if Mother had reached her limit. Her life waves were pulsing the world once again, but she was giving too much. The pulses became to intertwined that it began to blanket the world. That is when we became apart from Mother. In one last push of grace and mercy, Mother forced her children to the surface. She did not want us to disappear, but she knew that she could not protect us any longer. From the ruins of the old world, we rose from the blackened ground. The still burning earth stuck to our skin in order to give us form. The pulses became to breath life into the land restoring rivers, mountains, plains, and tundras to their former glory. And with her last breath, mother breathed life into her new children. Bring us, the Aurorian Australis, into the world. We were the protectors of this new world in Mother's stead. The earth that singed our skin proved out legitimacy. So as such we adopted the name "D'Synge" in reverence to the one true Mother.

Adaptation of D'Synge

Secluded by the surrounding waterways and large bodies of water, the D'Synge people have had to adapt to their area of occupation. The area is covered above by a sun-blocking canopy that led to the deformation of the eyes overtime and an enhancement in the ear cavity. Since the majority of the day the forest floor is blanketed by shade, the D'Synge people have learned to focus only what is moving whether it is in the distance of up-close. Their overall vision has not changed, but the focus of their eyes has.

The D'Synge people lead a life of modesty. Many of them are farmers and gatherers from the surrounding land. Though many are farmers many are forced into mandatory military service for the latter part of the year when the crops are not being tended to. The men are the bulk of the military force with women in positions of power. This comes fro the belief that women were made in the shape of their goddess of the Earth, Ameng'ta. This leads to women being more educated in the secular strategies and knowledge as a whole. Though some men are placed high in society for their natural aptitude for strategy.

The race as a whole thrives in the dark; this lead to the adaptation of dark magic into something unique to them. They have dubbed it Sih'Nah or Stealth Magic. This magic is used to cover the sound and tracks of the user as well as draw the attention of unsuspecting animals in order to ensnare them. But there are some who leave the village in order to envelope themselves in the secular world. Many of whom then become assassins for the highest bidder. Thus led to the creation of Sih'Rah or Assassination Magic. This magic focuses on the enhancement of the user making them faster, quieter, and at some times become one with the shadows.

  • Coming of Age Ceremony:

    • The Shadow Hound

      The D'Synge people are a very strict society. If one of the people in the tribe can not be useful, they are pushed into a rigorous training session by the oldest warrior. The D'Synge believe in the tribe being only as strong as their weakest member. The problem with this is that they also believe in a family setting in the tribe. Everyone watches out for one another in the tribe. This leads to a selection that is the exact opposite of natural selection. The weakest are revived from the bottom to become one of the strongest in their particular fields. The weak are chosen based on their Coming of Age ceremony, The Shadow Hound.

      The Shadow Hound is a ceremony in which the males and females, that are reaching the universal age of 21, converge on the center of the village. The chief meets alone with the new recruits into the tribe's ranks. The chief pulls out oracle bones in order to divine the route for the ceremony. After finding the route, the chief silently engraves the map into each particpants memory using her magic. The participants are then branded with their own brand belonging to them and them only. The brand comes from the chiefs magic forcing the dormant magical circuits of the participants. Infused with brand new overflowing vitality and agility, the participants dart into the forest. They each have their own mission to complete. The only person who knows each particpant's mission is the chief.

      As the recruits travel through the forest on their personal mission, the adults who attempted the ceremony the year before hunt. It is a tradition to weed out any who are not stealthy enough to complete a mission in which no one knows, but the person attempting the mission. The chief waits in the center of the village, meditating. Watching the entire forest for each participant and the completion of their mission. During the ceremony if the particpant's make any vocal noises, their magical circuits burst from abstaining from the lessons they were taught. The curse is lifted after the ceremony. Anyone who has their circuits destroyed are forced into being a hunter-gatherer or leaving the village from shame.

      The recruits who finish the trial are greeted with hugs and kisses from the entire village. The chief comes out of her trance overtaken by the spirits of the forest because of the sustained meditation. The possessed chief walks to each successful particpant and inscribes the tribe brand on a part of their body which correspondes with the field they are most suited for based on their recent performance. The people who failed to complete their personal mission but still have their magical circuits are pushed with the head of each field in order to rise above and redeem themselves by rising the ranks in their field's skill.
